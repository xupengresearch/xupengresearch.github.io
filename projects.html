Borderland Capitalism: the Chinese-run online scam industry in Southeast Asia

PI for this three-year project, Hallsworth Fellowship, University of Manchester, 2026–2029

This project examines the online scam industry and the wider political economy of digital illicit economies across Southeast Asia. It explores how scam operations are embedded in borderland governance, logistical systems, labour regimes, and cross-border infrastructures, and how they endure through adaptation, mobility, and regional reorganisation.

This broader research programme also informs my ongoing book project on the politics of digital illicit economies, logistics, and centre–periphery relations at the margins.
