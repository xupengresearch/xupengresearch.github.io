Research Overview

As a political scientist, my research examines how political and economic life is organised at the margins of the state and the market, with a particular focus on borderlands, illicit economies, and the transnational reach of Chinese capital. It is grounded in a long-standing engagement with borderlands as sites where sovereignty, mobility, and economic life are continuously negotiated. Over time, this broad interest developed into a more focused concern with the China–Myanmar borderland, where armed politics, illicit economies, and the transnational reach of Chinese capital converge in particularly concentrated form.
